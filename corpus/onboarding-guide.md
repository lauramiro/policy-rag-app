# New Employee Onboarding Guide

## Before Day One

HR sends a welcome packet 5 business days before the start date, including
a laptop shipping notification and instructions to complete Form I-9 and
tax withholding forms via the Workday portal. New hires should complete
these forms at least 2 business days before their start date.

### Background Check and I-9 Verification

Employment offers are contingent on successful completion of a background
check, initiated once the signed offer letter is received and typically
completed within 5 business days. Remote new hires complete Form I-9
identity verification through a scheduled video call with a notary
partner rather than in person, and must have original identity documents
ready for that call.

### Pre-Boarding for Remote New Hires

Remote new hires receive their laptop shipment 3 business days before
their start date (rather than being handed a device on day one) via a
tracked courier, along with a printed quick-start guide for initial VPN
and account setup steps to complete just before their first day.

## Day One Checklist

New employees attend a 2-hour orientation session covering benefits
enrollment, IT account setup, and an introduction to the employee
handbook. IT provisions email, Slack, and VPN access before 9:00 AM on
the start date. Badge and building access are issued at orientation.

### Buddy Program

Every new hire is paired with an onboarding buddy, an employee outside
their direct reporting line, before their start date. The buddy's role is
to answer informal questions, join the new hire for lunch or a virtual
coffee chat in the first week, and check in weekly for the first month.

### Role-Specific Day One Setup

Engineering new hires additionally receive access to source control, the
staging environment, and a repository walkthrough scheduled for their
first afternoon. Sales new hires receive CRM access and a shadowing
schedule for at least 2 client calls in their first week.

## First 30-60-90 Days

Managers must schedule a 30-day check-in to review onboarding progress, a
60-day check-in to set initial performance goals, and a 90-day review
that determines completion of the introductory period. Access to PTO and
the 401(k) match begins at the 90-day mark.

### 30-Day Check-In Focus

The 30-day check-in focuses on logistics: confirming all systems access
is working, reviewing completion of required training, and surfacing any
early blockers. It is informal and not part of the formal Performance
Review Policy rating process.

### 60-Day Check-In Focus

The 60-day check-in sets 2-3 initial performance goals for the remainder
of the introductory period and identifies any additional training or
mentorship needed to hit them, recorded in the performance platform for
reference at the 90-day review.

### 90-Day Review Outcomes

The 90-day review results in one of three outcomes: successful completion
of the introductory period (the default outcome), an extension of the
introductory period by up to 30 days if performance is inconclusive, or,
in rare cases, separation if performance is clearly below expectations.
Only successful completion unlocks PTO usage and the 401(k) match
described in the Benefits Overview.

## Required Training

All new hires must complete Security Awareness Training and Anti-
Harassment Training within their first 14 days, and Code of Conduct
training within 30 days. Managers must complete additional People
Manager training within 60 days of a new hire joining their team.

### Training Completion Tracking

Training completion is tracked in the Learning Management System (LMS)
and reported to each manager weekly. Employees who have not completed
Security Awareness Training within 14 days have VPN access automatically
suspended until the training is completed, consistent with the security
posture described in the Information Security Policy.

### Role-Specific Compliance Training

Employees in Finance additionally complete Anti-Bribery and Insider
Trading training within their first 30 days, referencing the standards in
the Code of Conduct. Employees with access to Restricted customer data
complete Data Privacy training within their first 14 days, aligned with
the Data Privacy Policy.

## Benefits Enrollment Window

New employees have a 30-day window from their start date to enroll in
health, dental, and vision benefits. Missing this window requires waiting
until the next open enrollment period unless a qualifying life event
occurs.

### Benefits Orientation Session

A dedicated benefits orientation session, separate from the day-one
general orientation, is scheduled within the new hire's first 2 weeks,
led by a benefits specialist who walks through plan tiers, the 401(k)
auto-enrollment default, and the wellness stipend described in the
Benefits Overview.

### Enrollment Confirmation

Once benefits elections are submitted, the new hire receives a
confirmation email within 3 business days summarizing their elections;
employees should review this confirmation carefully, as changes after the
30-day window closes require a qualifying life event as described in the
Benefits Overview.

## Manager Onboarding Responsibilities

Hiring managers are responsible for completing an onboarding readiness
checklist at least 3 business days before a new hire's start date,
confirming that equipment has been ordered, system access requests have
been submitted, the buddy has been assigned, and the first-week meeting
schedule has been built. Managers who have not completed this checklist
by the day before the start date receive an automated escalation to
their own manager.

### First-Week Manager Check-Ins

Beyond the buddy program, the direct manager holds a short check-in at
the end of day one and again at the end of the first week to answer
questions and confirm the new hire feels set up for success, separate
from the more structured 30-60-90 day check-ins described above.

## International New Hire Onboarding

New hires joining outside the country of the company's primary
operations follow a localized version of this guide, coordinated by a
regional HR partner, that substitutes the relevant country's statutory
onboarding requirements (such as local employment contract signature
and, where applicable, works council notification) for the Form I-9 and
Workday tax withholding steps described above, while preserving the same
day-one, 30-60-90, and training milestones.

## Onboarding Feedback Survey

New hires receive a short onboarding feedback survey at the end of their
first 30 days, distinct from the 30-day manager check-in, asking about
the clarity of the welcome packet, timeliness of equipment delivery, and
overall orientation quality. Results are aggregated by HR quarterly to
identify systemic onboarding gaps by department.
