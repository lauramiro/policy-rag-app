# Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy

## Accrual

Full-time employees accrue 15 PTO days per calendar year, credited at a
rate of 1.25 days per completed month of employment. Part-time employees
accrue PTO on a pro-rated basis according to their scheduled hours.
Employees become eligible to use accrued PTO after completing a 90-day
introductory period.

### Accrual by Tenure

Starting in an employee's fourth year of continuous service, the annual
accrual rate increases to 18 days per year (1.5 days per month), and in
the seventh year it increases again to 20 days per year (1.667 days per
month). Tenure-based increases take effect on the employee's work
anniversary and are reflected in the following month's accrual.

### Part-Time and Temporary Employee Accrual

Part-time employees scheduled for at least 20 hours per week accrue PTO
at the same 1.25 days-per-month rate, pro-rated by their scheduled hours
relative to a full-time 40-hour week (for example, an employee scheduled
for 20 hours per week accrues 0.625 days per completed month). Temporary
and seasonal employees engaged through a staffing agency are not eligible
for company PTO accrual and are instead subject to their staffing
agency's leave policies.

### Sick Leave

Sick leave is accrued separately from PTO at a rate of 1 hour per 30
hours worked, up to a maximum accrual of 48 hours per calendar year, and
is available for use immediately upon hire (it is not subject to the
90-day introductory period that applies to PTO). Sick leave may be used
for the employee's own illness, a family member's medical appointment, or
safe-time needs related to domestic violence, consistent with applicable
state paid sick leave laws.

## Requesting Time Off

Employees must submit PTO requests through the HR portal at least 5
business days in advance for requests longer than 2 days. Requests of 1-2
days may be submitted with 48 hours notice. Managers must approve or deny
requests within 3 business days. Unapproved requests are automatically
escalated to the department director after 5 business days of no
response.

### Blackout Periods

Certain teams (Finance during month-end and quarter-end close, Customer
Support during the two weeks around major product launches) may designate
blackout periods during which PTO requests require director-level
approval instead of standard manager approval. Blackout periods must be
published to the affected team at least 30 days in advance.

### Partial-Day and Extended Requests

Requests for partial-day PTO (less than 4 hours) can be submitted with 24
hours notice and do not require the standard approval workflow, only
manager notification. Requests for extended PTO of 10 or more consecutive
business days require submission at least 30 days in advance and a
written coverage plan approved by the manager.

## Carryover

Up to 5 unused PTO days may be carried over into the next calendar year.
Any unused days beyond that cap are forfeited on December 31st unless
local law requires payout. Carried-over days must be used by March 31st
of the following year or they are forfeited permanently.

### State-Specific Carryover Variations

In states that legally prohibit "use it or lose it" PTO policies
(including California and Montana), earned PTO is treated as a vested
wage benefit: no forfeiture cap applies, and all accrued, unused PTO
carries forward indefinitely and is paid out in full upon separation
regardless of the standard payout cap described below. HR maintains a
state-supplement table identifying which states this applies to.

### Sick Leave Carryover

Unlike PTO, unused sick leave carries over in full each year up to a bank
cap of 80 hours; sick leave does not have a use-by date once carried over.

## Unplanned Absences

Employees who are unable to work due to unforeseen circumstances should
notify their manager as soon as possible, ideally before the start of
their shift. Repeated unplanned absences may be addressed through the
performance review process. More than 4 unplanned absences in a rolling
90-day period triggers an automatic conversation with HR.

### Bereavement and Jury Duty Leave

Employees receive up to 5 paid bereavement days for the death of an
immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, sibling) and 3 paid days
for an extended family member, in addition to their PTO balance. Jury duty
and subpoenaed court appearances are paid in full for up to 10 business
days per year upon presentation of the court summons to HR; PTO is not
deducted for either category.

### Leave of Absence Coordination

Absences extending beyond 5 consecutive workdays for a medical or family
reason should be coordinated with HR as a formal Leave of Absence, which
may run concurrently with Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) protections
for eligible employees, rather than being drawn solely from the PTO
balance.

## Payout Upon Separation

Employees who separate from the company in good standing are paid out
for unused, accrued PTO up to a maximum of 10 days, calculated at their
final base hourly rate. Payout is issued on the next regular payroll
cycle following the last day of employment.

### Involuntary Termination and Payout

The 10-day payout cap applies equally to voluntary resignations and
involuntary terminations for reasons other than gross misconduct.
Employees terminated for gross misconduct (as defined in the Code of
Conduct) forfeit unused PTO payout to the extent permitted by state law.

### Negative PTO Balances

Employees who have taken PTO in advance of accruing it (with manager
approval) and separate from the company with a negative balance will have
the negative amount deducted from their final paycheck, subject to state
wage deduction limits and only with the employee's prior written
authorization on file.

## Interaction with the Holiday Schedule

PTO requests spanning a company-observed holiday automatically exclude
the holiday from the PTO deduction, consistent with the Holiday
Schedule's treatment of holidays that overlap approved leave; employees
booking time off around a holiday should still submit the full date
range so the calendar correctly reflects their planned absence.

## Manager Coverage Planning

For PTO requests longer than 5 consecutive business days, the requesting
employee's manager is responsible for confirming a coverage plan for any
time-sensitive responsibilities before approving the request. Managers
may ask the employee to identify a backup contact but may not deny a
otherwise-compliant request solely because a formal coverage plan has not
yet been finalized, provided the employee has given the required advance
notice.

## PTO Donation Program

Employees may voluntarily donate up to 5 accrued PTO days per calendar
year to a colleague experiencing a documented medical or family
emergency who has exhausted their own PTO and sick leave balances,
administered confidentially through HR. Donated days retain the cash
value of the donor's hourly rate converted to the recipient's rate for
payroll purposes, and donations are irrevocable once processed.
