# Performance Review Policy

## Review Cadence

Formal performance reviews occur twice per year, in June and December.
New employees receive an additional 90-day introductory review conducted
by their direct manager to confirm successful completion of onboarding.

## Rating Scale

Employees are rated on a 5-point scale: Exceeds Expectations, Strong
Performance, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, and Unsatisfactory.
Ratings of Needs Improvement or below automatically trigger a Performance
Improvement Plan (PIP) within 10 business days of the review.

## Performance Improvement Plans

A PIP runs for 60 calendar days and includes weekly check-ins between the
employee and manager. HR is looped in at the start of every PIP. At the
60-day mark, the manager documents whether performance has improved to a
Meets Expectations level or whether further action, including termination,
is warranted.

## Merit Increases and Promotions

Merit increases are tied to the December review cycle and take effect the
following January 1st. Promotion requests must be submitted by managers
at least 30 days before the review cycle and are approved by a
calibration committee to ensure consistency across teams.

## 360-Degree Feedback

For employees at the senior manager level and above, reviews incorporate
360-degree feedback from at least 3 peers and 2 direct reports, collected
confidentially through the performance platform 4 weeks before the review
meeting.
