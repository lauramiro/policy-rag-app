# Performance Review Policy

## Review Cadence

Formal performance reviews occur twice per year, in June and December.
New employees receive an additional 90-day introductory review conducted
by their direct manager to confirm successful completion of onboarding.

### Mid-Cycle Check-Ins

In addition to the formal June and December reviews, managers are expected
to hold informal 1:1 check-ins at least monthly to discuss progress
against goals, so that neither the manager nor the employee is surprised
by feedback at the formal review. These check-ins are not documented in
the formal performance platform.

### Goal Setting

Employees set quarterly goals (OKRs) in the performance platform within
the first 2 weeks of each quarter, in alignment with their manager and
team objectives. Goal attainment feeds into, but does not solely
determine, the overall rating at the June and December review cycles.

## Rating Scale

Employees are rated on a 5-point scale: Exceeds Expectations, Strong
Performance, Meets Expectations, Needs Improvement, and Unsatisfactory.
Ratings of Needs Improvement or below automatically trigger a Performance
Improvement Plan (PIP) within 10 business days of the review.

### Self-Assessment

Prior to each formal review, employees complete a written
self-assessment covering their goal attainment, key accomplishments, and
areas for growth, submitted at least 1 week before the manager finalizes
the rating. Managers must reference the self-assessment in their written
review, even where they disagree with the employee's self-rating.

### Rating Distribution Guidance

To promote consistency across teams, the calibration committee reviews
the distribution of ratings across each department and flags departments
where Exceeds Expectations ratings exceed 25% of the team or
Unsatisfactory ratings are disproportionately concentrated in a single
manager's reports, without mandating a strict forced curve.

## Performance Improvement Plans

A PIP runs for 60 calendar days and includes weekly check-ins between the
employee and manager. HR is looped in at the start of every PIP. At the
60-day mark, the manager documents whether performance has improved to a
Meets Expectations level or whether further action, including termination,
is warranted.

### PIP Structure

Every PIP document includes 3-5 specific, measurable objectives, the
support the company will provide (additional training, mentorship,
adjusted workload), and the explicit consequence of not meeting the
objectives. The employee acknowledges receipt of the PIP in writing,
though acknowledgment does not require agreement with its contents.

### Appeals Process

An employee placed on a PIP may request a review of the decision by HR
and the manager's own manager (skip-level) within 5 business days of
receiving the PIP, if they believe the rating leading to it was applied
inconsistently with this policy. The appeal does not pause the 60-day PIP
clock unless HR determines a procedural error occurred.

## Merit Increases and Promotions

Merit increases are tied to the December review cycle and take effect the
following January 1st. Promotion requests must be submitted by managers
at least 30 days before the review cycle and are approved by a
calibration committee to ensure consistency across teams.

### Merit Increase Ranges by Rating

Merit increase guidance ranges from 0% for Unsatisfactory or Needs
Improvement ratings, 2-4% for Meets Expectations, 4-7% for Strong
Performance, and 7-12% for Exceeds Expectations, subject to the
department's overall merit budget for that cycle set by Finance.

### Off-Cycle Promotions

Off-cycle promotions outside the December cycle are permitted only for
significant, sudden scope changes (such as an unplanned team lead
vacancy) and require both the calibration committee's approval and VP
sign-off, rather than the standard committee-only approval used for the
December cycle.

## 360-Degree Feedback

For employees at the senior manager level and above, reviews incorporate
360-degree feedback from at least 3 peers and 2 direct reports, collected
confidentially through the performance platform 4 weeks before the review
meeting.

### Selecting 360 Feedback Providers

The reviewed employee proposes a list of feedback providers, which their
manager may adjust to ensure a balanced perspective (including at least 1
provider who does not directly work with the employee daily). Feedback
responses are anonymized in the summary shared with the employee, though
raw responses are visible to the reviewing manager and HR.

### Using 360 Feedback in Ratings

360-degree feedback informs the manager's assessment of collaboration and
leadership competencies but does not mechanically determine the final
rating; the manager retains ultimate accountability for the rating,
consistent with the standard 5-point scale used for all other employees.

## Documentation Standards

Every formal review must be documented in the performance platform with
specific, evidence-based examples supporting the rating, rather than
general statements; managers are expected to cite at least one concrete
example per competency area rated below Meets Expectations. Reviews
lacking sufficient documentation are returned to the manager by HR before
being finalized and communicated to the employee.

## Manager Calibration Training

Before each formal review cycle, all people managers complete a
calibration training session covering rating consistency, unconscious
bias, and how to write actionable feedback. Managers who have not
completed the training within 10 business days of the cycle opening are
required to have their draft ratings co-reviewed by their own manager
before finalization.

## Career Ladder and Leveling

Ratings and promotion decisions are made with reference to the
company's published career ladder, which defines scope, impact, and
expected competencies at each level (e.g., Associate, Senior, Staff,
Principal for individual contributors; Manager, Senior Manager,
Director, VP for people leaders). Promotion to a new level requires
sustained performance at the next level's expectations for at least 2
consecutive review cycles, except in the off-cycle promotion scenarios
described above.

## Remote Employee Review Considerations

For fully remote employees, managers are expected to supplement direct
observation with explicit check-ins and written status updates when
assessing collaboration-related competencies, since informal in-office
visibility is unavailable; the Remote Work Policy's provision that
remote status must not affect ratings applies equally here.

## Handling Manager Changes Mid-Cycle

When an employee changes managers partway through a review cycle (due to
reorganization or a manager's departure), the outgoing and incoming
managers jointly prepare the review, with the outgoing manager providing
written input for the portion of the cycle they supervised. If the
outgoing manager is unavailable, HR facilitates gathering peer input to
fill the gap rather than leaving that portion of the cycle unassessed.

## Underperformance Outside the Formal Cycle

Managers are not required to wait for the June or December review to
address a significant, acute performance concern; a PIP may be initiated
at any time during the year following the same 60-day structure
described above, with the outcome documented and factored into the next
formal review rather than replacing it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can an employee dispute their rating without invoking the formal PIP
appeals process?** Yes; any employee may request a follow-up
conversation with their manager and, if unresolved, with HR, to
discuss disagreement with a rating, whether or not that rating resulted
in a PIP.

**Are ratings shared with anyone beyond the employee and manager?**
Ratings are visible to HR, the calibration committee, and the manager's
own manager for calibration purposes, but are not shared broadly across
the organization.

**Does a Leave of Absence during the review period affect the
rating?** Managers are expected to prorate goal expectations for
employees who were on an approved leave for a significant portion of the
review period, rather than penalizing them for reduced output during
that time.
