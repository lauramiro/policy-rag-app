# Learning and Development Policy

## Purpose

The company invests in employee growth through an annual learning budget,
tuition reimbursement, conference attendance, and internal mentorship.
This policy explains what is available, who is eligible, and how to access
each benefit.

## Annual Learning Budget

### Amount

Each full-time employee receives an annual professional development budget
of $1,500 per calendar year. Part-time employees scheduled for at least 20
hours per week receive a pro-rated budget of $750. Unused budget does not
carry over to the next calendar year and is forfeited on December 31st.

### Eligible Expenses

The budget may be applied to online courses, professional certifications,
books, industry memberships, and workshops that are relevant to the
employee's current role or a documented career path within the company.
Expenses are submitted through the standard expense process; see the
Expense Policy for reimbursement timelines and receipt requirements.

## Tuition Reimbursement

### Eligibility

Employees who have completed 12 months of continuous employment may apply
for tuition reimbursement for accredited degree programs relevant to the
company's business. Applications require manager and department-director
approval before enrollment.

### Reimbursement Amount

The company reimburses 80% of tuition costs up to a maximum of $5,250 per
calendar year, contingent on the employee earning a grade of B (3.0) or
better, or a "pass" in a pass/fail course. Reimbursement is paid after
final grades are submitted to HR.

### Repayment Obligation

Employees who voluntarily leave the company within 12 months of receiving
tuition reimbursement must repay a pro-rated portion of the reimbursed
amount, calculated at 1/12 forgiven for each full month of continued
employment after the reimbursement date.

## Conferences and External Events

Employees may request to attend up to one major industry conference per
year, subject to manager approval and budget availability. Registration,
travel, and lodging for approved conferences are covered separately from
the annual learning budget and are booked and expensed per the Travel
Policy. Attendees are expected to share key takeaways with their team
within 2 weeks of returning.

## Internal Learning

### Mentorship Program

The company runs a voluntary mentorship program matching employees with
senior colleagues for structured career guidance. Mentorship pairings run
in 6-month cycles, and participation is tracked as a development goal in
the Performance Review Policy.

### Learning Time

Employees may allocate up to 4 hours per week of paid work time toward
approved learning activities, provided current project commitments are
met and the time is coordinated with their manager in advance.

## Records and Certifications

HR maintains a record of completed certifications and degrees on each
employee's profile. Certifications required for a role (for example,
security or compliance credentials) are fully company-funded outside the
annual learning budget and must be kept current as a condition of the
role.
