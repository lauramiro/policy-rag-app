# Parental and Family Leave Policy

## Overview

This policy describes paid and unpaid leave available to employees for the
birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child, and for the care of a
seriously ill family member. It runs alongside, and where applicable
concurrently with, protections under the federal Family and Medical Leave
Act (FMLA) and any more generous state or local family-leave laws.

## Paid Parental Leave

### Eligibility

Employees who have completed 6 months of continuous full-time employment
are eligible for paid parental leave. Part-time employees scheduled for at
least 20 hours per week are eligible on a pro-rated basis.

### Amount and Duration

Eligible birthing parents receive 16 weeks of paid parental leave at 100%
of base salary. Non-birthing parents, including adoptive and foster
parents, receive 8 weeks of paid parental leave at 100% of base salary.
Leave must be taken within 12 months of the child's birth or placement.

### Intermittent and Staggered Leave

With manager approval, up to 4 weeks of an employee's paid parental leave
entitlement may be taken intermittently in blocks of no fewer than 5
consecutive business days during the 12-month eligibility window, rather
than all at once. The remaining entitlement must be taken as a continuous
block.

## Family Care Leave

Employees may take up to 12 weeks of job-protected leave in a rolling
12-month period to care for a spouse, domestic partner, child, or parent
with a serious health condition. The first 4 weeks of family care leave
are paid at 60% of base salary; the remainder is unpaid but job-protected.
Employees may substitute accrued PTO to supplement unpaid portions.

## Interaction with Other Policies

### PTO Accrual During Leave

PTO continues to accrue during the paid portion of any leave under this
policy. PTO does not accrue during unpaid, extended leave beyond 12 weeks.
See the Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy for accrual details.

### Benefits Continuation

Company-sponsored medical, dental, and vision coverage continues
unchanged during approved leave, with the company covering its normal
share of premiums. See the Benefits Overview for plan details.

## Requesting Leave

Employees should notify HR and their manager at least 30 days before a
foreseeable leave start date, or as soon as practicable for an
unforeseeable event. Requests are submitted through the HR portal with
supporting documentation (an expected due date, adoption placement letter,
or a health-care provider's certification for family care leave).

## Return to Work

Employees returning from leave are reinstated to the same or an equivalent
position with equivalent pay and benefits. Nursing parents are provided a
private lactation room and reasonable break time for up to 12 months
following the child's birth. Employees may request a phased return of up
to 2 weeks at a reduced schedule, coordinated with their manager.
