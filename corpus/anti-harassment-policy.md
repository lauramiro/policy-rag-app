# Anti-Harassment and Anti-Discrimination Policy

## Prohibited Conduct

The company prohibits harassment or discrimination based on race, color,
religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity, national origin, age,
disability, or veteran status. This includes unwelcome verbal, physical,
or visual conduct that creates an intimidating, hostile, or offensive work
environment, whether in the office, remote, or at company events.

### Examples of Prohibited Conduct

Prohibited conduct includes, but is not limited to: derogatory jokes or
slurs based on a protected characteristic, unwelcome sexual advances or
requests for favors, displaying offensive images or materials (including
in virtual backgrounds or shared screens during video calls), and
persistent unwanted messages on Slack or other company communication
tools after being asked to stop. A single severe incident, such as an
unwanted physical touch, can constitute harassment even without a pattern
of repeated conduct.

### Third-Party Harassment

This policy also protects employees from harassment by non-employees,
including vendors, contractors, and customers. Employees who experience or
witness harassment by a third party should report it through the same
channels described below; the company will address the conduct with the
third party's employer or terminate the business relationship if
necessary.

## Reporting Channels

Employees may report harassment to their manager, any HR representative,
or the confidential ethics hotline at 1-800-555-0199, available 24/7. If
the concern involves the employee's manager, the report should go
directly to HR or a skip-level manager instead.

### Manager Responsibilities Upon Receiving a Report

Any manager who receives a harassment complaint, witnesses harassing
conduct directly, or becomes aware of a credible allegation must report
it to HR within 24 hours, regardless of whether the affected employee
has asked that no action be taken. Failure by a manager to escalate a
known complaint is itself a violation of this policy and subject to
discipline up to termination.

### Anonymous Reporting

Reports made through the ethics hotline may be submitted anonymously.
While anonymity can limit the scope of the investigation (for example, if
follow-up questions cannot be asked), HR will investigate anonymous
reports to the fullest extent the available information allows.

## Investigation Process

HR must begin an investigation within 3 business days of receiving a
report. Investigations typically conclude within 30 calendar days,
though complex cases may take longer with the reporter notified of
delays. Both the complainant and respondent are informed of the outcome
in writing.

### Investigation Steps

A typical investigation includes separate interviews with the
complainant, the respondent, and any identified witnesses, a review of
relevant written communications (email, Slack) where appropriate and
proportionate, and a written summary of findings prepared by HR or an
external investigator for complex or senior-level matters. All
investigation records are kept confidential and stored separately from
the employee's general personnel file.

### Discipline Following a Substantiated Finding

Where an investigation substantiates a violation, discipline ranges from
a documented warning to immediate termination depending on severity,
following the progressive discipline framework described in the Code of
Conduct, except that conduct involving physical contact, threats, or
repeated violations after a prior warning typically results in
termination regardless of position or tenure.

## Interim Protective Measures

While an investigation is ongoing, HR may implement interim measures such
as schedule changes, reporting-line changes, or paid administrative leave
to protect the complainant. These measures are not considered disciplinary
action against either party.

### Scope of Interim Measures

Interim measures may also include restricting the respondent's access to
certain communication channels or work areas, temporary remote-work
requirements, or a "no contact" directive between the parties. HR
reassesses the need for interim measures at least every 2 weeks while the
investigation remains open.

## Non-Retaliation

Retaliation against anyone who reports harassment in good faith or
participates in an investigation is strictly prohibited and is grounds
for immediate termination, regardless of the outcome of the underlying
investigation.

### Recognizing Retaliation

Retaliation can include demotion, undesirable schedule changes, exclusion
from meetings the employee would normally attend, negative performance
reviews issued without legitimate basis following a report, or subtle
social exclusion by a manager or team. Employees who believe they are
experiencing retaliation should report it through the same channels used
for the original harassment report.

### External Reporting Rights

Employees always retain the right to file a charge with the Equal
Employment Opportunity Commission (EEOC), a state fair employment
agency, or an equivalent body outside the United States, in addition to
or instead of using internal reporting channels, and the company's
internal timelines described above do not extend or limit any external
filing deadline.

## Training Requirements

All employees complete Anti-Harassment Training within their first 14
days of employment, as referenced in the Onboarding Guide, and a
refresher course annually thereafter, due each year by the employee's
hire-date anniversary. Managers and anyone with hiring authority
additionally complete a Manager Harassment Response training within 60
days of stepping into a people-leadership role, covering how to receive
a report, the duty to escalate within 24 hours, and how to avoid common
investigative missteps.

### Training Delivery and Recordkeeping

Training is delivered through the Learning Management System (LMS) and
must be completed with a passing score on the accompanying knowledge
check; employees who do not pass on the first attempt are given one
additional attempt within 5 business days. Completion records are
retained for the duration of employment plus 3 years to support
compliance recordkeeping obligations in applicable states.

## Documentation and Recordkeeping

All complaints, investigation notes, and outcomes are documented in a
confidential HR case management system separate from the employee's
general personnel file, accessible only to HR, Legal, and, where
necessary, the assigned investigator. Records of substantiated
complaints are retained for 7 years; records of complaints that were
investigated but not substantiated are retained for 3 years.

### Bystander Intervention

Employees who witness conduct that may violate this policy, even if not
directed at them, are encouraged (though not required) to intervene
safely in the moment if appropriate, and are always expected to report
what they witnessed using the reporting channels above. Bystanders who
report in good faith receive the same non-retaliation protections as a
direct complainant.

## Global and Contractor Coverage

This policy applies to all employees regardless of country of
employment, as well as to contractors, interns, and temporary staff
while performing work for the company. Where local law provides greater
protection or a shorter investigation timeline than described above (for
example, certain EU member states), the more protective local standard
controls for employees in that jurisdiction.
