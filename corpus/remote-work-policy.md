# Remote Work Policy

## Eligibility

Employees are eligible for remote work after completing 6 months of
continuous employment and receiving written manager approval. Roles
classified as "on-site required" (facilities, front-desk reception, and
lab technicians) are not eligible regardless of tenure.

### Requesting Remote Work Status

Employees request remote status through the HR portal, including their
proposed primary work location and arrangement type (fully remote or
hybrid). Managers must respond within 10 business days, and denials must
include a documented business reason, such as team collaboration needs
or the role's on-site-required classification.

### Trial Period for New Remote Arrangements

The first 60 days of any new fully-remote arrangement are treated as a
trial period; either the employee or manager may revert to the prior
arrangement during this window without it being treated as a denial of a
standing right, provided at least 10 business days' notice is given.

## Work Location and Hours

Remote employees must designate a primary work location on file with HR
and update it within 5 business days of any change. Core collaboration
hours are 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM in the employee's local time zone; outside
of core hours, employees may flex their schedule as long as they complete
40 hours per week.

### Working from a Different State or Country Temporarily

Employees who wish to work temporarily from a different state or country
(for example, while visiting family) for up to 20 business days per
calendar year may do so with manager notification but without a formal
location change, provided the temporary location does not create tax or
legal complications flagged by Legal for that specific country. Stays
longer than 20 business days require a formal Digital Nomad request
reviewed by HR, Legal, and Payroll due to potential tax withholding and
right-to-work implications.

### Time Zone Accommodation

Employees whose designated work location is more than 3 time zones from
their team's primary hub are exempted from the standard 10:00 AM-3:00 PM
core hours requirement and instead agree on a custom overlap window of at
least 3 hours with their manager, documented in the HR system.

## Home Office Stipend

The company provides a one-time home office setup stipend of $500,
reimbursable through the expense system within the first 60 days of
approved remote status. An additional $30/month stipend is provided for
internet and utilities, paid via payroll.

### Ergonomic Assessment

Remote employees may request a complimentary virtual ergonomic assessment
with an occupational health consultant within their first 90 days of
remote status. Equipment recommended by the assessment (an ergonomic
chair, monitor arm, or keyboard tray) is reimbursable up to an additional
$300 beyond the standard $500 setup stipend, submitted through the same
expense process within 60 days of the assessment.

### Stipend for Hybrid Employees

Hybrid employees (in-office 2 or more days per week) receive a
prorated home office stipend of $250 (half the fully-remote amount) and
are not eligible for the $30/month internet stipend, since the company
office serves as their primary internet-connected workspace on scheduled
in-office days.

## Equipment and Security

Remote employees must use company-issued laptops with full-disk
encryption enabled and must connect to internal systems only via the
company VPN. Use of personal devices to access production systems is
prohibited. Home Wi-Fi networks must use WPA2 or WPA3 encryption.

### Shared Living Spaces and Confidentiality

Employees working from a shared living space (roommates, co-working
space) must position their screen so that Restricted data, as defined in
the Information Security Policy, is not visible to others, and must use
headphones for calls discussing confidential customer or financial
information.

## In-Office Expectations

Employees on a hybrid arrangement must be in the office a minimum of 2
days per week, coordinated with their team lead. Fully remote employees
are expected to attend in-person all-hands events quarterly, with travel
expenses covered per the Travel Policy.

### Communication Norms for Distributed Teams

Remote and hybrid employees are expected to keep their Slack status
accurate to reflect availability, respond to direct messages within 4
business hours during their working day, and default to recorded video
meetings for any discussion where a remote colleague's time zone prevents
live attendance.

### Performance Expectations

Remote work status does not change performance expectations or the
Performance Review Policy's rating scale; managers evaluate remote
employees on the same goals and cadence as in-office employees and may
not penalize a rating solely on the basis of remote status.

## Office Desk Booking for Hybrid Employees

Employees on a hybrid arrangement reserve a desk through the office
hoteling system at least 1 business day in advance of an in-office day;
teams with 3 or more hybrid members are additionally assigned a
shared neighborhood of desks to preserve in-person collaboration
density. No-shows for a reserved desk on 3 or more occasions in a
rolling 30-day period may result in the reservation privilege being
suspended for that employee for 2 weeks.

## Team Offsite Attendance

In addition to the quarterly company all-hands referenced above, teams
may hold their own in-person offsites up to twice per year; attendance
is expected for both fully remote and hybrid team members unless
excused by the manager, with travel booked and expensed per the Travel
Policy. Employees with a documented medical, disability, or caregiving
accommodation need may request a virtual attendance alternative,
reviewed by HR on a case-by-case basis.

## Relocation While Remote

Remote employees who relocate permanently, whether to a new city, state,
or country, must update their designated work location with HR at least
30 days before the move (rather than the standard 5-business-day update
window for minor changes) so that Payroll, Legal, and Benefits can assess
tax withholding, right-to-work, and benefits-plan network changes before
the move takes effect. A move to a state or country where the company
does not have an established legal entity may require the employee to
transition to contractor status or, in some cases, may not be
approvable; HR will advise within 10 business days of the relocation
request.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I work from a coffee shop or co-working space instead of my
registered home address?** Yes, occasionally, provided the Shared
Living Spaces and Confidentiality requirements above are followed and
the location still falls within the employee's designated work
location for tax purposes; a co-working space used as a primary,
regular work location should be added as the designated address on
file with HR.

**Does the $30/month internet stipend cover a second monitor's
electricity cost or other utilities beyond internet?** The stipend is
intended to offset a reasonable estimate of incremental internet and
utility costs from working at home and is not itemized against specific
bills; employees do not need to submit separate utility receipts.

**What happens to my remote status if I'm promoted into a people
manager role?** Remote eligibility is tied to the role classification,
not seniority; a promotion does not automatically change remote
status unless the new role is itself classified as on-site required, in
which case HR will discuss a transition plan with the employee before
the promotion takes effect.

## Equipment Refresh for Remote Employees

Remote employees follow the same equipment refresh cadence described in
the Equipment and Offboarding Policy (an upgrade every 3 years, or sooner
with manager approval), with replacement equipment shipped to the
employee's designated work location using the same prepaid logistics
used for offboarding equipment returns.
