# Employee Code of Conduct

## Professional Standards

Employees are expected to treat colleagues, customers, and vendors with
respect and professionalism at all times. This includes prompt, honest
communication and avoiding conflicts of interest. Any outside employment
or board position must be disclosed to HR within 10 business days of
accepting it.

### Outside Employment Review

Once disclosed, HR and the employee's manager jointly review outside
employment or board positions for conflicts with the company's business,
use of company time, or competitive overlap. Approval or denial is
communicated within 10 business days of disclosure; undisclosed outside
employment discovered later is treated as a Code of Conduct violation
regardless of whether an actual conflict exists.

### Workplace Behavior Expectations

Employees are expected to arrive prepared for meetings, respond to
business-critical messages within one business day, and give credit
accurately for shared work. Disagreements about work product should be
raised directly with the relevant colleague or, if unresolved, escalated
to a manager rather than discussed only with uninvolved third parties.

## Conflicts of Interest

Employees may not accept gifts valued over $75 from vendors or clients
without disclosing them to their manager and Compliance. Financial
relationships with direct reports (loans, business partnerships) must be
disclosed and may result in reassignment to avoid supervisory conflicts.

### Gift Giving

Employees giving gifts to vendors, clients, or government officials on
behalf of the company are subject to the same $75 threshold and must log
gifts over that amount with Compliance in advance. Gifts to government
officials of any value require pre-approval from Legal due to
anti-corruption law restrictions.

### Anti-Bribery and Anti-Corruption

Consistent with the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) and the UK
Bribery Act, employees may never offer, promise, or authorize anything of
value to a government official, customer, or partner to improperly
influence a business decision. Facilitation payments to expedite routine
government action are prohibited without exception, even in jurisdictions
where locally customary.

### Insider Trading

Employees who have access to material non-public information about the
company or its business partners may not trade in those securities or
share the information ("tipping") with others until the information has
been publicly disclosed. Designated insiders are subject to quarterly
trading blackout windows communicated by Legal.

## Confidential Information

Employees must not disclose confidential company information, including
unreleased product plans, financial results, or customer data, to anyone
outside the company. This obligation survives termination of employment
for a period of 2 years under the standard employment agreement.

### Social Media and Public Communications

Employees may identify their employer on personal social media but must
include a disclaimer that views are their own when discussing
company-related topics. Employees may not share unreleased product
information, screenshots of internal tools, or confidential financial
data on any public platform. Only designated spokespeople may speak to
media or press on the company's behalf.

### Media and Speaking Engagements

Requests from press, analysts, or conference organizers for interviews or
public speaking on behalf of the company must be routed through
Corporate Communications for approval at least 5 business days before any
commitment is made.

## Use of Company Resources

Company equipment, software licenses, and email accounts are provided for
business use. Limited personal use is permitted as long as it does not
interfere with job duties, violate other policies, or consume excessive
bandwidth or storage (over 5 GB of personal files).

### Substance Use in the Workplace

Employees may not be under the influence of alcohol or illegal drugs
while performing job duties, operating company vehicles, or on company
premises, except at company-sanctioned events where alcohol is served
responsibly. Reasonable suspicion of impairment may result in a
requirement to submit to testing consistent with applicable law, and a
refusal is treated as a policy violation.

### Political Activity

Employees may engage in personal political activity on their own time
using personal resources, but may not use company email, letterhead, or
equipment to solicit political contributions or endorse candidates in a
way that implies company endorsement. The company does not reimburse
personal political contributions.

## Reporting Violations

Employees who witness a violation of this Code should report it to their
manager, HR, or the anonymous ethics hotline at 1-800-555-0199. Reports
are reviewed by the Ethics Committee within 5 business days and
retaliation against a good-faith reporter is grounds for termination.

### Progressive Discipline

For conduct issues that do not warrant immediate termination, the company
generally follows a progressive discipline model: verbal warning, written
warning, final written warning with a Performance Improvement Plan where
applicable, and termination. Serious violations, including harassment,
theft, safety violations, or fraud, may result in immediate termination
without progressing through earlier steps.

### External Reporting Rights

Nothing in this Code restricts an employee's right to report suspected
legal violations to a government agency, including the SEC, EEOC, or
OSHA, or to participate in a resulting investigation, and no
non-disclosure obligation in this Code or any agreement overrides that
right.

## Workplace Safety and Health

Employees must immediately report any workplace injury, unsafe
condition, or near-miss to their manager and Facilities, regardless of
severity. Serious injuries requiring medical attention beyond first aid
must be reported within 4 hours so the company can meet OSHA recordable
injury reporting timelines. Weapons of any kind are prohibited on
company premises or at company events, except where carried by
licensed security personnel in the course of their duties.

### Workplace Violence

Threats of violence, physical altercations, or intimidating behavior
toward a colleague, customer, or vendor are grounds for immediate
termination and, where appropriate, referral to law enforcement.
Employees who feel unsafe due to another person's behavior should
contact Security or the ethics hotline immediately rather than waiting
for a scheduled HR meeting.

## Global Trade Compliance

Employees involved in shipping products, software, or technical data
across borders must comply with U.S. export control laws (EAR, ITAR)
and equivalent import/export regulations in other countries of
operation, including screening counterparties against denied-party
lists before finalizing an international sale or data transfer.
Questions about whether a specific transaction requires an export
license should be directed to Legal at least 10 business days before
the transaction is expected to close.

## Dress Code

The company follows a "dress for your day" standard: business casual
attire is expected for in-office work and client-facing meetings, while
casual attire is acceptable for individual-contributor work on non-client
days. Client-facing roles and employees attending external events should
follow the more formal standard appropriate to the audience, at their
manager's discretion.

## Conflict of Interest Disclosure Process

Employees complete an annual Conflict of Interest disclosure through the
Compliance portal, in addition to the ad hoc 10-business-day disclosure
requirement for new outside employment or board positions described
above. The annual disclosure covers financial interests in competitors,
family relationships with vendors, and any other circumstance that could
reasonably appear to affect the employee's objectivity. Compliance
reviews all disclosures within 15 business days and follows up directly
with the employee where clarification is needed.
