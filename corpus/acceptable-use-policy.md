# Acceptable Use Policy

## Scope

This policy governs the acceptable use of company technology resources,
including laptops, mobile devices, email, messaging tools, internet access,
and cloud applications. It applies to all employees, contractors, and
interns. It complements, and does not replace, the Information Security
Policy, which governs technical security controls.

## General Principles

Company technology resources are provided primarily for business purposes.
Limited, reasonable personal use is permitted provided it does not
interfere with work duties, consume significant resources, or violate any
company policy. Employees have no expectation of privacy in company systems;
the company may monitor and audit use of its resources consistent with
applicable law.

## Prohibited Uses

### Content and Conduct

Employees must not use company resources to create, store, or transmit
material that is unlawful, harassing, discriminatory, or defamatory, or
that infringes intellectual property rights. Conduct that would violate the
Code of Conduct or Anti-Harassment Policy offline is equally prohibited on
company systems.

### Security-Related Prohibitions

Employees must not disable or circumvent security controls, share their
account credentials, connect unauthorized devices to the corporate
network, or attempt to access data or systems for which they lack
authorization. Suspected security incidents must be reported per the
Information Security Policy.

## Software and Installation

### Approved Software

Employees may install software only from the company's approved software
catalog. Installing software from outside the catalog requires an IT
exception request, which is reviewed for security and licensing
compliance. Unlicensed or "cracked" software is strictly prohibited.

### Cloud and AI Tools

Use of third-party cloud services or generative AI tools with company data
is permitted only for services on the approved list. Restricted or
Confidential data, as defined in the Information Security Policy, must not
be entered into any unapproved external tool.

## Email and Communications

Employees must use company email for business communications and must not
auto-forward company email to personal accounts. Mass emails to
distribution lists require manager approval. Employees should apply the
same professionalism to internal messaging tools as to email.

## Personal Devices (BYOD)

Employees who access company email or applications from a personal mobile
device must enroll the device in the company's mobile device management
(MDM) system, which enforces a screen-lock passcode and enables remote
wipe of company data if the device is lost or the employee leaves. Personal
devices must not be used to access production systems; see the Remote Work
Policy for remote-access requirements.

## Enforcement

Violations of this policy may result in suspension of access privileges
and disciplinary action up to and including termination, consistent with
the Code of Conduct. Suspected criminal activity may be referred to law
enforcement.
