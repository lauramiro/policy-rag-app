# Employee Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Submission Process

Employees must submit expense reports through the Expensify portal within
30 calendar days of incurring the expense. Reports submitted after 60
days will not be reimbursed except with VP-level approval. Each line item
requires an itemized receipt for amounts over $25.

### Missing Receipts

If an itemized receipt is lost, the employee may submit a Missing Receipt
Affidavit through Expensify for amounts under $75; amounts over $75
without a receipt require manager and Finance approval and are limited to
2 occurrences per employee per calendar year before additional scrutiny
applies.

### Corporate Card Program

Employees who travel or incur expenses regularly (more than $500/month
on average) are issued a corporate card through the Finance team.
Corporate card transactions must still be coded and receipted in
Expensify within the standard 30-day window even though the company pays
the card issuer directly; personal charges on a corporate card must be
reimbursed to the company within 15 days of the statement date.

## Approval Limits

Direct managers may approve expense reports up to $500 per report.
Reports between $500 and $2,500 require director approval, and anything
above $2,500 requires VP of Finance sign-off. Approvals must occur within
5 business days of submission or the report auto-escalates.

### Delegated Approval During Absences

If an approver is unavailable (on PTO or travel) for more than 3 business
days, Expensify automatically routes pending approvals to their
designated backup approver, who must be identified in the system before
any planned absence longer than 3 business days.

### Split and Recurring Expenses

Expenses that recur monthly (such as a work-related software
subscription not otherwise covered by IT-procured tools) may be set up as
a recurring reimbursement pre-approved by the manager for up to 12
months, rather than requiring a fresh report each month, provided the
monthly amount stays under $100.

## Meals and Entertainment

Meal reimbursement while traveling is capped at $75 per day ($15
breakfast, $20 lunch, $40 dinner). Client entertainment expenses require
the names and companies of all attendees to be listed and are capped at
$150 per person.

### Team Meals and Off-Sites

Internal team meals (not involving clients), such as a team lunch to
celebrate a launch, are capped at $40 per person and require director
approval if the total exceeds $500. Team off-site events must be
budgeted and approved by the department head at least 30 days in
advance.

### Alcohol at Client Entertainment

A moderate amount of alcohol at client entertainment events is
reimbursable within the $150 per-person cap, but the company reserves the
right to decline reimbursement for excessive alcohol expenses judged
disproportionate to the business purpose, at Finance's discretion.

## Non-Reimbursable Items

The company does not reimburse alcohol for solo meals, traffic or parking
fines, personal entertainment, spa or gym charges, or first-class airfare
upgrades. Any submission containing non-reimbursable items will be
returned to the employee for correction rather than partially processed.

### Mileage and Personal Vehicle Use

Employees who use a personal vehicle for business travel (not including
a normal commute) are reimbursed at the current IRS standard mileage
rate, submitted through Expensify with the trip's starting and ending
odometer readings or a mapping tool printout showing the route and
distance.

### Conference and Training Expenses

Registration fees, required materials, and associated travel for
job-related conferences or training approved in advance by the
employee's manager are reimbursable under this policy and are not subject
to the $2,500 director-approval threshold if the conference itself was
already approved as part of an annual training budget.

## Reimbursement Timeline

Approved expense reports are paid out within 2 pay cycles (typically 10
business days) via direct deposit alongside regular payroll. Corporate
card charges are reconciled automatically and do not require separate
reimbursement.

### International Expenses and Currency Conversion

Expenses incurred in a foreign currency are converted to the employee's
home payroll currency using the exchange rate on the date of purchase, as
sourced automatically by Expensify; employees do not need to manually
calculate conversions but should retain the original foreign-currency
receipt.

### Expense Audits

Finance conducts random quarterly audits of expense reports across all
approval tiers to verify compliance with this policy. Employees found to
have submitted fraudulent or knowingly non-compliant expenses are subject
to discipline up to termination under the Code of Conduct and may be
required to repay reimbursed amounts.

## Relocation Expenses

Employees who relocate for a company-initiated transfer or a new hire
who relocates as a condition of an offer are eligible for a relocation
package covering moving company costs, up to 30 days of temporary
housing, and one house-hunting trip for the employee and spouse or
partner, subject to a package cap agreed in writing before the move and
approved by the hiring VP. Relocation expenses reimbursed above the IRS
tax-free moving expense limit (currently $0 for non-military moves under
federal tax law) are grossed up for tax purposes at the company's
discretion.

## Per Diem for Extended Domestic Assignments

Employees on a domestic assignment away from their home location for
more than 14 consecutive days may elect a daily per diem of $65 (covering
all meals) in lieu of itemizing individual meal receipts, in place of the
standard $75 daily meal cap with breakfast/lunch/dinner sub-limits
described above. Per diem elections must be made before the assignment
begins and cannot be changed mid-assignment.

## Approval Exceptions for Legal and M&A Matters

Expenses related to active legal matters or M&A due diligence may be
pre-approved in bulk by the General Counsel for the duration of the
matter, bypassing the standard per-report approval limits, provided each
individual expense is still itemized and receipted in Expensify in the
normal manner for audit purposes.

## Client Gifts and Marketing Expenses

Client gifts follow the $75 disclosure threshold described in the Code
of Conduct; gifts over $75 require Compliance sign-off in addition to
the standard expense approval chain. Marketing and business-development
expenses such as sponsorships or trade show booths follow the same
approval tiers as other expenses but are budgeted separately and coded
to the marketing cost center rather than the traveling employee's team
budget.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What happens if I submit an expense report more than 60 days after
the expense with no VP approval on file?** The report is automatically
rejected by Expensify and the employee must escalate to their VP
directly if they believe an exception is warranted; Finance does not
process late reports without documented VP sign-off.

**Can I combine multiple small purchases into a single line item?**
No; each purchase should be itemized separately even if from the same
vendor on the same day, so that category-specific caps (such as the
daily meal caps) can be validated automatically by Expensify.

**Who do I contact if my reimbursement hasn't arrived within the
2-pay-cycle window?** Employees should first confirm the report shows
as "Approved" in Expensify, then contact payroll@company.example if
payment has not appeared within 3 business days of the expected date.
