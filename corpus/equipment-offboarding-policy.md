# Equipment and Offboarding Policy

## Equipment Issuance

New employees are issued a laptop, monitor, and peripherals within their
first 3 business days, tracked by asset tag in the IT inventory system.
Employees may request a laptop upgrade every 3 years or sooner with
manager approval if the device no longer meets performance needs.

### Standard Equipment Kits

The standard kit includes a laptop (MacBook Pro or equivalent Windows
device based on role), one external monitor, a keyboard, mouse, and
headset. Engineering roles are additionally issued a second monitor by
default. Employees requesting non-standard equipment (a second monitor
outside engineering, a standing desk converter, ergonomic accessories)
submit a request through the IT portal, approved by their manager, with
delivery typically within 10 business days.

### Loss, Damage, and Theft

Equipment lost, damaged, or stolen while in an employee's possession must
be reported to IT within 1 hour, consistent with the reporting window in
the Information Security Policy, so that any accounts and data on the
device can be remotely wiped. A police report is required for stolen
equipment claims. The first incident of accidental damage per rolling
12-month period is repaired or replaced at no cost to the employee;
subsequent incidents within the same 12-month window may be charged at a
prorated replacement cost.

## Resignation Notice

Employees are asked to provide at least 2 weeks of written notice before
their last day. Employees in engineering leadership roles are asked to
provide 4 weeks of notice to support knowledge transfer and hiring
continuity.

### Knowledge Transfer Expectations

During the notice period, departing employees work with their manager to
complete a knowledge transfer document covering active projects, key
contacts, and access to any team-owned systems not centrally managed by
IT. For individual contributor engineering roles, a minimum 1-hour
handoff session with the incoming owner of key projects is expected
before the last day where a successor has been identified.

### Rescinding a Resignation

An employee who wishes to rescind a resignation during the notice period
may request to do so, but reinstatement is not automatic and requires
approval from the employee's manager and HR, considering factors such as
whether a replacement has already been hired.

## Offboarding Checklist

On an employee's last day, IT must revoke VPN, email, and application
access by 5:00 PM, and HR must conduct an exit interview. Managers are
responsible for collecting all company equipment, including laptops,
badges, and any company-owned mobile devices, before the employee departs.

### Involuntary Termination Procedures

For involuntary terminations, IT access is revoked immediately at the
start of the termination meeting rather than at end of day, to reduce
risk of data exfiltration or system tampering. HR schedules the exit
interview, where appropriate, within 5 business days after the
termination rather than on the final day itself.

### Software License Deprovisioning

In addition to revoking core access (VPN, email, applications), IT must
deprovision named-user software licenses (design tools, analytics
platforms, developer tools) within 24 hours of the last day to avoid
unnecessary license renewal costs, and must remove the departing
employee from any shared cloud infrastructure accounts or admin consoles.

## Equipment Return

Departing employees must return all company equipment within 5 business
days of their last day, using a prepaid shipping label provided by IT for
remote employees. Equipment not returned within 30 days is charged to the
employee at replacement cost, deducted from final pay where permitted by
law.

### International Employee Equipment Return

For employees based outside the country of the company's primary
operations, IT coordinates a courier pickup rather than a standard
shipping label to account for customs and import documentation, and the
30-day return window is extended to 45 days to accommodate international
shipping timelines.

## Final Pay and Benefits Continuation

Final paychecks, including accrued PTO payout, are issued according to
state law, typically within the next regular pay cycle. Departing
employees receive COBRA continuation information for health benefits
within 14 days of their termination date.

### Rehire Eligibility

Employees who leave in good standing are generally eligible for rehire
and may reapply for open roles at any time; former employees are flagged
in the applicant tracking system with their separation reason and manager
feedback to inform future hiring decisions. Employees terminated for
cause, including Code of Conduct or Anti-Harassment Policy violations,
are marked ineligible for rehire.

### Retirement-Specific Offboarding

Employees separating due to retirement may, upon request submitted at
least 60 days before their last day, retain access to company alumni
resources (retiree health plan information, invitations to select company
events) that are not extended to employees who separate for other
reasons.

## Equipment for Contractors and Temporary Staff

Contractors and temporary staff engaged for more than 30 days may be
issued a company laptop at the discretion of the engaging manager, but
are not eligible for the standard full-time equipment kit (dual
monitors, ergonomic accessories) described above. Contractor equipment
must be returned on the earlier of the engagement end date or 5 business
days after the last invoiced day of work, using the same prepaid
shipping process available to remote employees.

## Exit Survey and Feedback

Departing employees are invited, though not required, to complete a
confidential exit survey administered by HR, separate from the exit
interview, covering reasons for leaving, manager effectiveness, and
suggestions for improvement. Aggregated, anonymized exit survey themes
are reported to department leadership quarterly; individual responses
are not shared with the departing employee's direct manager.

## Access Provisioning Timeline Recap

For reference, the standard equipment and access timeline runs as
follows: equipment issued within 3 business days of a new hire's start
date (Equipment Issuance, above); VPN, email, and application access
revoked by 5:00 PM on a voluntary departure's last day, or immediately
at the start of the termination meeting for an involuntary departure
(Offboarding Checklist, above); and named-user software licenses
deprovisioned within 24 hours of the last day (Offboarding Checklist,
above). Any deviation from this timeline for a specific department must
be documented and approved by IT leadership.

## Data Handling During Offboarding

Before a departing employee's access is revoked, their manager, in
coordination with IT, must ensure any files stored locally on the
employee's device or in personal cloud folders that contain company
work product are transferred to a shared team location. Company email
and Slack history are retained per the retention schedules in the Data
Privacy Policy rather than being deleted at offboarding, since they may
contain business records needed after the employee departs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What happens if I cannot return my equipment within 5 business days
due to shipping delays outside my control?** Employees should notify
IT before the deadline; documented shipping delays (carrier backlog, a
declared weather emergency) pause the 30-day charge-at-replacement-cost
clock until the delay is resolved.

**Do I need to wipe my own data before returning a laptop?** No;
IT performs a full wipe and reimaging of all returned equipment as
standard procedure, so employees do not need to manually delete files,
though they should ensure any personal files they want to keep have
already been backed up elsewhere before shipping the device.

**Is severance pay affected by equipment return timing?** No; equipment
return and final pay are handled as separate processes, though
unreturned equipment charges described above may still be deducted from
final pay where permitted by law even after severance has been paid.
