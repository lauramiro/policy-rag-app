# Employee Benefits Overview

## Health Insurance

The company offers three medical plan tiers (Bronze, Silver, Gold)
through Anthem, with the company covering 80% of the employee premium and
50% of dependent premiums on the Silver plan. Dental and vision coverage
are fully employer-paid for the employee-only tier.

### Plan Tier Comparison

The Bronze plan carries a $2,500 individual deductible and a lower
monthly premium, making it best suited to employees who rarely use
in-network care. The Silver plan, the company's benchmark plan for
premium subsidy calculations, carries a $1,000 individual deductible.
The Gold plan carries a $250 individual deductible and the highest
monthly premium, with the company still covering 80% of the employee-only
premium but a smaller share (35%) of the dependent premium given its
richer coverage. All three tiers cap employee out-of-pocket maximums at
$6,000 individual / $12,000 family per calendar year.

### Dependent Eligibility

Spouses, domestic partners (with a signed affidavit on file with HR), and
children up to age 26 are eligible for dependent coverage regardless of
student or marital status. Disabled dependents over age 26 may remain on
the plan indefinitely with annual proof of continued disability submitted
to the benefits team by January 31st.

### Open Enrollment and Qualifying Life Events

Open enrollment runs for two weeks each November, with elections taking
effect January 1st. Outside of open enrollment, employees may change
elections only after a qualifying life event (marriage, divorce, birth or
adoption, loss of other coverage) and must submit supporting documentation
within 30 days of the event.

## Retirement (401k)

The company matches 100% of employee 401(k) contributions up to 4% of
base salary, and 50% of the next 2% contributed, for a maximum match of
5% of salary. Employer match contributions vest immediately; there is no
vesting schedule.

### Contribution Options and Limits

Employees may contribute on a pre-tax, Roth (after-tax), or combined
basis, subject to the annual IRS elective deferral limit. Contribution
elections can be changed at any time through the Fidelity portal and take
effect on the next available payroll cycle, typically within 2 pay
periods. Employees aged 50 and over are eligible for catch-up
contributions per IRS limits.

### Automatic Enrollment and Escalation

New employees who do not make an active election are automatically
enrolled at a 3% pre-tax contribution rate starting on their first
paycheck after the 90-day introductory period, with automatic 1% annual
increases each January up to a cap of 8%, unless the employee opts out or
changes their rate. Employees may opt out of auto-enrollment at any time
before the first contribution is deducted.

### Investment Options and Financial Advice

The plan offers a menu of 18 index and target-date funds. Employees may
schedule a complimentary 30-minute session with a Fidelity financial
advisor once per calendar year at no cost, arranged through the benefits
portal.

## Parental Leave

Eligible employees receive 12 weeks of paid parental leave at 100% of
base salary for the birth, adoption, or fostering of a child. Leave must
be taken within 12 months of the qualifying event and can be split into
two blocks of at least 2 weeks each.

### Eligibility and Notice

Parental leave is available to employees who have completed at least 6
months of continuous employment as of the qualifying event. Employees
should provide at least 30 days' advance notice when the leave is
foreseeable (such as an expected birth or a planned adoption placement);
for unforeseeable events, notice should be given as soon as practicable.

### Coordination with Disability and State Leave

Birth mothers recovering from childbirth may combine parental leave with
short-term disability benefits under this Benefits Overview, subject to a
physician's certification of recovery time, typically 6-8 weeks for a
vaginal delivery and 8 weeks for a cesarean delivery. In states with a
paid family leave program, company parental leave pay is coordinated with
(not stacked on top of) state benefits so that total pay does not exceed
100% of base salary.

### Secondary/Non-Birth Parent Leave

Non-birth parents, including adoptive and foster parents and partners of
a birth parent, are eligible for the same 12 weeks at 100% pay described
above; the policy does not differentiate leave length by parental role.

## Life and Disability Insurance

The company provides basic life insurance at 1x annual salary at no cost
to the employee, with supplemental coverage available for purchase up to
5x salary. Short-term disability covers 60% of salary for up to 12 weeks;
long-term disability covers 60% of salary after a 90-day elimination
period.

### Accidental Death and Dismemberment (AD&D)

Basic AD&D coverage equal to the basic life insurance amount (1x salary)
is provided at no cost and pays out for covered accidental injuries or
death, in addition to any basic life insurance benefit. Supplemental
AD&D can be purchased alongside supplemental life insurance in the same
enrollment window.

### Beneficiary Designations

Employees must designate at least one primary beneficiary for life and
AD&D coverage during benefits enrollment; failure to designate a
beneficiary results in payout to the estate by default. Beneficiary
designations can be updated at any time through the benefits portal and
should be reviewed after major life events such as marriage or divorce.

### Long-Term Disability Claim Process

Employees anticipating a long-term disability claim should notify the
benefits team as early as possible during the short-term disability
period so that the transition at the 90-day elimination period is
seamless. Long-term disability benefits continue until recovery, return
to work, or age 65, whichever comes first, subject to periodic medical
recertification every 12 months.

## Wellness Stipend

Employees receive an annual $600 wellness stipend, reimbursable for gym
memberships, fitness equipment, or mental health app subscriptions.
Unused stipend amounts do not carry over to the following year.

### Eligible and Ineligible Expenses

In addition to gym memberships and fitness equipment, the stipend covers
nutrition counseling, therapy co-pays not covered by insurance, wearable
fitness trackers, and race entry fees. It does not cover spa services,
massage memberships, or general wellness products such as supplements.

### Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Separately from the wellness stipend, all employees and household members
have access to a free Employee Assistance Program providing up to 6
confidential counseling sessions per issue per year, legal consultation
referrals, and financial planning support, available 24/7 through a
dedicated EAP hotline.

### Commuter and Transit Benefits

Employees who commute to an office may enroll in a pre-tax commuter
benefits account for transit passes and qualified parking, up to the IRS
monthly pre-tax limit. Elections can be adjusted monthly to reflect
changes in commuting patterns, including for hybrid employees under the
Remote Work Policy.

## Tuition and Professional Development

Employees may be reimbursed up to $5,250 per calendar year (the current
IRS tax-free educational assistance limit) for tuition, certification
exam fees, and required course materials related to their current role
or a reasonably anticipated future role at the company. Reimbursement
requires a grade of B or better (or "Pass" in a pass/fail course) and
manager pre-approval before the course begins. Amounts reimbursed above
the IRS limit in a given year are treated as taxable income.

### Professional Development Stipend

Separately from tuition reimbursement, employees receive an annual $1,000
professional development stipend for conferences, books, online courses,
or professional association memberships directly relevant to their role,
approved by their manager and submitted through the standard expense
process described in the Expense Policy. Unused stipend does not roll
over to the next year.

## Additional Voluntary Benefits

### Adoption and Fertility Assistance

In addition to the 12 weeks of parental leave described above, the
company reimburses up to $10,000 in qualified adoption expenses (agency
fees, legal fees, travel for adoption-related travel) and up to $10,000
in qualified fertility treatment expenses not covered by the medical
plan, each available once per employee lifetime, submitted with
supporting documentation to the benefits team.

### Pet Insurance and Legal Plan

Employees may purchase discounted voluntary pet insurance and a group
legal plan (covering will preparation, real estate transactions, and
consultation on personal legal matters) at group rates through payroll
deduction. These voluntary benefits are fully employee-paid and are not
subsidized by the company, unlike the core medical, dental, and vision
plans described above.

### Benefits for Part-Time Employees

Part-time employees scheduled for at least 20 hours per week are
eligible for dental and vision coverage and 401(k) participation
(including the standard match formula) but are not eligible for
company-subsidized medical coverage, basic life insurance, or the
wellness stipend, which are reserved for employees scheduled 30 or more
hours per week.
