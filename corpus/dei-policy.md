# Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Policy

## Commitment

The company is committed to building a diverse workforce and an inclusive
workplace where every employee can contribute fully. This policy describes
our equal opportunity commitments, accommodation processes, and the
programs that support an inclusive culture.

## Equal Employment Opportunity

The company provides equal employment opportunity to all employees and
applicants without regard to race, color, religion, sex, sexual
orientation, gender identity, national origin, age, disability, veteran
status, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law. This
commitment applies to hiring, promotion, compensation, training,
discipline, and all other terms and conditions of employment.

## Inclusive Hiring

### Structured Interviews

Hiring teams use structured interviews with consistent, role-relevant
questions and a scoring rubric to reduce bias. Every interview panel for a
role should include at least two trained interviewers.

### Diverse Candidate Slates

For manager-level and above openings, recruiting aims to present a slate
that includes qualified candidates from underrepresented groups before a
final selection is made. Slate composition does not override the
requirement to hire the best-qualified candidate.

## Reasonable Accommodations

### Disability Accommodations

Employees and applicants with disabilities may request reasonable
accommodations through HR. The company engages in an interactive process to
identify an effective accommodation and provides it unless doing so would
impose an undue hardship. Ergonomic and workspace accommodations are
coordinated with the Workplace Health and Safety Policy.

### Religious Accommodations

Employees may request accommodations for sincerely held religious beliefs,
including schedule adjustments for observances or exceptions to dress and
grooming standards, which the company grants absent undue hardship.

## Employee Resource Groups

The company supports voluntary, employee-led Employee Resource Groups
(ERGs) organized around shared identities or experiences. ERGs receive a
modest annual budget and executive sponsorship. Participation is open to
all employees, including allies, and is voluntary.

## Pay Equity

The company conducts an annual pay-equity analysis to identify and correct
unexplained pay disparities across protected groups for comparable roles.
Findings are reviewed by senior leadership, and corrective adjustments are
made as warranted.

## Reporting Concerns

Employees who experience or witness discrimination should report it through
the channels described in the Anti-Harassment Policy and Code of Conduct.
The company prohibits retaliation against anyone who raises a good-faith
concern or participates in an investigation.

## Training

All employees complete inclusion and anti-bias training within 60 days of
hire and annually thereafter. People managers complete additional training
on inclusive management and the accommodation process.
