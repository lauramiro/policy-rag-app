# Data Privacy Policy

## Scope and Data Types

This policy governs handling of personal data belonging to employees,
customers, and job applicants, including names, contact details, payment
information, and government identifiers such as Social Security numbers.
Personal data is classified as Restricted under the Information Security
Policy.

### Roles and Responsibilities

The Privacy team, led by the Data Protection Officer (DPO), owns this
policy and is the point of contact for regulators and data subjects. Each
department that processes personal data (Sales, Support, People/HR,
Finance) designates a Privacy Champion responsible for maintaining an
accurate record of processing activities for their function, reviewed
annually with the Privacy team.

### Employee Monitoring Disclosure

The company may monitor corporate email, Slack, and network activity on
company-issued devices for security and compliance purposes. Employees
are notified of this monitoring during onboarding and by a banner
displayed at login; monitoring does not extend to personal devices except
for the company-managed partition described in the Information Security
Policy's BYOD provisions.

## Data Minimization and Retention

Customer support tickets containing personal data are retained for 24
months after case closure, then automatically purged. Job applicant data
for unsuccessful candidates is retained for 12 months to comply with
equal-opportunity recordkeeping requirements, then deleted.

### Additional Retention Schedules

Employee personnel records are retained for 7 years after separation to
satisfy tax, benefits, and employment-law recordkeeping obligations.
Marketing contact data (newsletter subscribers, webinar registrants) is
retained until the individual unsubscribes or, if inactive, for a maximum
of 36 months, after which it is purged in a quarterly cleanup process.
Financial transaction records are retained for 7 years to satisfy audit
and tax requirements.

### Data Minimization Principles

Teams collecting new categories of personal data must complete a Data
Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) with the Privacy team before launch
whenever the collection involves Restricted data at scale, special
category data (health, biometric), or automated decision-making that
significantly affects individuals. The DPIA confirms that only data
necessary for the stated purpose is collected.

## Data Subject Requests

Individuals may submit access, correction, or deletion requests to
privacy@company.example. The Privacy team must acknowledge the request
within 3 business days and complete fulfillment within 30 calendar days,
consistent with GDPR and CCPA timelines.

### Verifying Requester Identity

Before fulfilling an access or deletion request, the Privacy team must
verify the requester's identity using at least two matching data points
(such as email address and account number) to prevent fraudulent
requests. Requests that cannot be verified are denied with an explanation
and an alternative verification path offered.

### CCPA-Specific Rights

California residents additionally have the right to opt out of the "sale"
or "sharing" of personal information (the company does not sell personal
data but does share some data with advertising partners, so an opt-out
link is provided on the website footer) and the right to non-discrimination
for exercising privacy rights. CCPA requests to know or delete are
fulfilled within the same 30-day CCPA-aligned timeline referenced above,
with one 15-day extension available for complex requests.

### GDPR-Specific Rights

For individuals covered by GDPR, the rights supported include access,
rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, and
objection to processing based on legitimate interest. Requests related to
automated decision-making with legal effect may also require human review
of the decision upon request.

## Third-Party Data Sharing

Personal data may only be shared with third-party vendors that have
signed a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) and completed the annual
security questionnaire. Marketing may not share customer email lists with
external partners without the Privacy team's written approval.

### International Data Transfers

Transfers of personal data from the EU or UK to vendors in the United
States or other countries without an adequacy decision must rely on
Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) incorporated into the vendor's DPA,
supplemented by a transfer impact assessment for high-risk vendors.

### Sub-Processor Notification

Vendors that engage sub-processors to help deliver their service must
notify the Privacy team at least 30 days before adding a new
sub-processor that will handle company personal data, giving the Privacy
team an opportunity to object.

## Breach Notification

In the event of a confirmed data breach involving personal data, the
Privacy and Security teams must notify affected individuals within 72
hours of confirming the breach's scope, and notify relevant regulators
within the timeframes required by applicable law (e.g., 72 hours under
GDPR).

### Breach Response Coordination

Data breaches are handled jointly by the Privacy team and the Security
team's incident response process described in the Information Security
Policy; a data breach that also qualifies as a SEV1 or SEV2 security
incident triggers both the security post-incident review and the privacy
breach-notification workflow in parallel, not sequentially.

### Employee Notification Obligations

Any employee who suspects personal data has been sent to the wrong
recipient, lost, or accessed without authorization must notify
privacy@company.example within 1 hour of discovery, mirroring the
1-hour reporting window in the Information Security Policy, even if they
are unsure whether a reportable breach occurred; the Privacy team makes
the final determination.

## Website Tracking and Cookies

The company's public websites use cookies and similar tracking
technologies classified into three categories: strictly necessary
(always on), analytics (on by default, opt-out available), and
advertising/marketing (off by default, requiring opt-in consent in
jurisdictions that require it, such as the EU under the ePrivacy
Directive). A cookie preference center is linked in the website footer
and preferences are honored within 24 hours of a change.

### Children's Data

The company's products are not directed at children under 13 (or under
16 in jurisdictions with a higher age of digital consent, such as
certain EU member states), and the company does not knowingly collect
personal data from children below the applicable age. Any account
discovered to belong to an underage user is suspended and associated
data deleted within 30 days of discovery, consistent with COPPA
requirements.

## Vendor Risk Tiering

Vendors that process personal data are assigned a risk tier at
onboarding: Tier 1 (access to Restricted data at scale, such as a cloud
hosting provider) requires an annual security questionnaire and a
right-to-audit clause in the DPA; Tier 2 (limited access to Internal or
a small volume of Restricted data) requires the questionnaire every 2
years; Tier 3 (no access to personal data) requires only standard
procurement review. Re-tiering occurs automatically if a vendor's
service scope changes materially.

## Litigation Hold Exception to Retention

The standard retention schedules described above (24 months for support
tickets, 12 months for unsuccessful applicant data, 7 years for
personnel and financial records) are suspended for any data subject to
a litigation hold issued by Legal. Data under a litigation hold is
retained until Legal issues a written release, even if the standard
retention period would otherwise have expired.
