# Workplace Health and Safety Policy

## Purpose

The company is committed to providing a safe and healthy workplace for all
employees, whether they work on-site or remotely. This policy describes
safety responsibilities, incident reporting, emergency procedures, and
ergonomic support.

## Responsibilities

### Company Responsibilities

The company maintains facilities in compliance with applicable
occupational health and safety regulations, provides required safety
equipment, and ensures that emergency exits, fire suppression, and first
aid supplies are available and inspected on schedule.

### Employee Responsibilities

Employees must follow safety procedures, use provided equipment correctly,
keep their work areas free of hazards, and promptly report unsafe
conditions. Employees must not work under the influence of alcohol or
non-prescribed drugs, consistent with the Code of Conduct.

## Incident and Injury Reporting

### Reporting Timeline

Any workplace injury, illness, or near-miss must be reported to the
employee's manager and to HR within 24 hours of the incident, regardless
of severity. Serious injuries requiring emergency care must be reported
immediately after care is obtained.

### Documentation

HR records each reported incident in the safety log and, where required by
law, files the appropriate regulatory report. The employee, manager, and a
safety representative complete an incident review for any injury resulting
in medical treatment beyond first aid, identifying corrective actions.

## Emergency Procedures

### Evacuation

Each office floor has posted evacuation routes and designated assembly
points. The company conducts fire evacuation drills at least twice per
year. Employees must familiarize themselves with the nearest exits and the
location of fire extinguishers and first aid kits.

### Medical Emergencies

Trained first-aid and CPR responders are identified on each floor. In a
medical emergency, employees should call emergency services first, then
notify a first-aid responder and building security.

## Ergonomics

### On-Site Workstations

Employees may request an ergonomic assessment of their on-site workstation
through HR. Recommended adjustments and equipment (adjustable chairs,
monitor risers, keyboard trays) are provided at company expense.

### Remote Workstations

Remote employees are eligible for a virtual ergonomic assessment and
related equipment reimbursement as described in the Remote Work Policy.
Employees are responsible for maintaining a safe home work environment.

## Workplace Violence Prevention

The company prohibits threats, intimidation, and violence in the
workplace. Employees who observe threatening behavior should report it
immediately to security and HR. Credible threats are escalated to law
enforcement. Retaliation against an employee who reports a safety or
violence concern in good faith is prohibited.

## Communicable Illness

Employees who are ill should stay home and use sick leave as described in
the PTO Policy. During a declared public-health event, the company may
implement additional measures, such as enhanced cleaning, remote-work
requirements, or health screenings, communicated through official
channels.
