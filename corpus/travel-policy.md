# Business Travel Policy

## Booking Process

All business travel must be booked through the Concur travel portal at
least 7 business days before departure for domestic trips and 14 business
days for international trips. Exceptions for urgent travel require
director approval and must be documented in the booking notes.

### Combining Business and Personal Travel

Employees may extend a business trip for personal travel (a "bleisure"
trip) as long as any additional personal-day flight cost difference is
paid by the employee and documented in Concur, and the traveler notifies
Security if the personal extension is international, per the
International Travel Requirements below. The company does not cover
lodging, meals, or ground transportation for the personal portion of the
trip.

### Travel Loyalty Programs

Employees may retain personal airline and hotel loyalty points earned on
company-booked travel. However, employees may not select a more expensive
flight or hotel option solely to earn additional loyalty benefits when a
lower-cost, policy-compliant option is available.

## Airfare Class

Economy class is standard for flights under 6 hours. For flights over 6
hours, employees at director level and above may book Premium Economy.
Business class requires VP approval regardless of flight duration.

### Medical Exceptions to Airfare Class

Employees with a documented medical condition requiring additional space
or accommodation may request an exception to standard airfare class
through HR and the travel team, supported by a note from a healthcare
provider, without needing to meet the director-level threshold otherwise
required for Premium Economy.

## Lodging Limits

Hotel spend is capped at $250/night for domestic travel in standard
cities and $350/night in designated high-cost cities (New York, San
Francisco, London, Tokyo). Bookings above the cap require pre-approval
from the traveler's manager.

### Extended Stay Travel

For business trips exceeding 14 consecutive nights, travelers should book
extended-stay or corporate housing options rather than standard hotels
where available in the destination city, as these typically offer a lower
effective nightly rate; Concur surfaces extended-stay options
automatically for trips of this length.

### Conference Hotel Exceptions

When attending a conference with an official conference hotel priced
above the standard nightly cap, booking the conference hotel is permitted
without additional pre-approval, provided the rate does not exceed the
cap by more than 25% and the conference hotel offers a meaningful
convenience benefit (shuttle-free access, networking events).

## Ground Transportation

Rideshare and taxi expenses are reimbursable for airport transfers and
client meetings. Rental cars require a mid-size or smaller vehicle unless
transporting equipment or more than 3 colleagues, in which case an SUV may
be booked.

### Personal Vehicle Use for Travel

Employees who choose to drive their personal vehicle to a destination
instead of flying are reimbursed at the standard IRS mileage rate
referenced in the Expense Policy, capped at the equivalent cost of the
lowest available economy airfare for the same trip, whichever is lower.

## International Travel Requirements

Employees traveling internationally must register their trip with the
Security team at least 5 business days in advance and confirm passport
validity of at least 6 months beyond the return date. The company
provides emergency travel assistance coverage through a third-party
provider for all approved international trips.

### Visa and Entry Documentation

Visa application fees, expedited passport renewal fees (when travel is
company-required with less than 60 days' notice), and required
vaccinations for the destination country are reimbursable business
expenses, submitted through the standard Expense Policy process with the
international trip's approval attached.

### Per Diem for Incidentals

For international trips, travelers may elect a daily incidental per diem
of $15/day (covering tips, laundry, and small incidentals) in lieu of
itemizing these small receipts individually, in addition to the standard
meal caps described in the Expense Policy.

### Emergency Procedures While Traveling Internationally

Travelers experiencing a medical emergency, natural disaster, or civil
unrest while on an approved international trip should contact the
third-party emergency assistance provider first for immediate support,
then notify the Security team and their manager as soon as safely
possible; the Security team maintains a 24/7 on-call rotation for
traveler emergencies mirroring the security hotline described in the
Information Security Policy.

## Group and Offsite Travel Booking

When 5 or more employees travel to the same destination for a team
offsite or conference, the travel team coordinates group booking
directly with the airline and hotel to secure group rates, rather than
each employee booking individually through Concur. Group travel
organizers should contact the travel team at least 21 business days
before departure to allow time for group rate negotiation.

## Travel Advances

Employees traveling to a destination where corporate cards are not
widely accepted, or on an extended assignment of more than 21 days, may
request a travel advance of up to $1,000 through Payroll, reconciled
against actual expenses submitted in Expensify within 15 days of
returning; any unspent advance balance is deducted from the employee's
next paycheck.

## Travel Safety Training

Employees who travel internationally more than twice per year, or to a
destination flagged as elevated-risk by the company's travel security
provider, are required to complete a 30-minute travel safety briefing
before their next trip, covering local emergency contacts, situational
awareness, and how to reach the 24/7 security on-call rotation described
above.

## Non-Reimbursable Travel Expenses

Consistent with the Expense Policy's general non-reimbursable items,
travel-specific items that are never reimbursable include travel
insurance purchased directly through a booking site (the company's
third-party emergency assistance coverage already applies to approved
trips), airline seat upgrade fees beyond the approved airfare class, and
in-flight entertainment or Wi-Fi purchases for personal use unrelated to
business communication.

## Trip Cancellation and Changes

Trips canceled or changed within 48 hours of departure for a
business reason (client reschedule, project change) are booked as
non-refundable fares whenever the fare difference is smaller than the
refundable fare premium, and any resulting change fee is a reimbursable
business expense. Trips canceled for personal reasons unrelated to
business need are the employee's responsibility unless covered by the
third-party travel insurance associated with the booking.

## Roles and Responsibilities Summary

The traveler is responsible for booking within policy limits and
submitting expenses within the standard 30-day window described in the
Expense Policy. The approving manager is responsible for verifying the
business purpose and cost reasonableness before approval. The travel
team is responsible for negotiating group rates, maintaining the
approved hotel and airline vendor list, and administering the
international trip registration process described above. The Security
team is responsible for reviewing international trip registrations and
maintaining the 24/7 emergency on-call rotation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I book a flight outside Concur if I find a better price?**
Generally no; Concur negotiated rates already reflect corporate
discounts, and bookings made outside Concur are reimbursed only up to
what the equivalent Concur fare would have cost, with the difference
being the employee's responsibility.

**What if my flight is delayed and I miss a connecting business
meeting?** Notify your manager and, for client-facing meetings, the
client contact as soon as the delay is known; rebooking costs caused by
airline delays (not personal preference) are fully reimbursable.

**Do I need approval to add a rental car to an existing trip?**
Ground transportation additions under $200 do not require separate
pre-approval beyond the original trip approval, but must still comply
with the mid-size-or-smaller vehicle standard described above.
