# Company Holiday Schedule

## Observed Holidays

The company observes 10 paid holidays per year: New Year's Day, Martin
Luther King Jr. Day, Memorial Day, Juneteenth, Independence Day, Labor
Day, Thanksgiving Day, the day after Thanksgiving, Christmas Eve, and
Christmas Day. Holiday pay is calculated at the employee's standard daily
rate.

### New Hire Holiday Eligibility

New employees are eligible for paid holidays starting on their first day
of employment; there is no waiting period for holiday pay, unlike PTO
which requires completion of the 90-day introductory period before use.
An employee whose start date falls on or after a given holiday is not
paid for that holiday in their first pay period.

### Holidays Overlapping Other Leave

If an observed holiday falls within an approved PTO, sick leave, or
parental leave period, the day is recorded as a holiday rather than
drawing down the employee's leave balance, so employees effectively
"get the day back" in their PTO or sick leave bank.

## Floating Holidays

In addition to the 10 fixed holidays, full-time employees receive 2
floating holidays per calendar year to use for religious observances or
personal occasions. Floating holidays must be scheduled at least 3
business days in advance through the HR portal and are forfeited if
unused by December 31st.

### Religious Accommodation Beyond Floating Holidays

Employees whose religious practice requires observance of holidays not
covered by the fixed schedule or the 2 floating holidays may request
additional unpaid or PTO-based accommodation under the company's
religious accommodation process, coordinated with HR, consistent with
Title VII obligations to provide reasonable accommodation absent undue
hardship.

### Pro-Rated Floating Holidays for New Hires

Employees who start after July 1st in a given calendar year receive 1
floating holiday (rather than 2) for that partial year, pro-rated based
on start date, and receive the full 2 floating holidays beginning the
following January 1st.

## Winter Office Closure

The office closes between December 24th and January 1st each year. Days
falling within this closure that are not designated holidays are treated
as company-paid days off and do not count against PTO balances.

### Essential Personnel During Closure

Employees in roles designated as essential (on-call infrastructure
engineers, customer support covering critical accounts) may be asked to
remain reachable during the winter closure. Essential personnel who are
called in to work during the closure receive an additional paid day off
to be used within 60 days, on top of their standard holiday pay.

## Holidays Falling on Weekends

When an observed holiday falls on a Saturday, it is observed on the
preceding Friday. When it falls on a Sunday, it is observed on the
following Monday. Employees on a rotating shift schedule receive
equivalent holiday pay or a substitute day off within 30 days.

### International and Regional Holiday Variations

Employees based outside the United States observe the statutory public
holidays of their home country in place of the U.S. holiday schedule
listed above, as published in an annual regional holiday calendar
maintained by HR for each country of operation. Where a country's
statutory holiday count is fewer than 10, the difference is added as
additional floating holidays; where it is greater, the local schedule
controls without reduction.

## Holiday Pay for Hourly Employees

Non-exempt hourly employees who work on an observed holiday receive 1.5x
their standard hourly rate in addition to their regular holiday pay,
consistent with the premium pay provisions in this schedule.

### Holiday Pay Calculation Examples

For an hourly employee whose standard rate is $25/hour who works an
8-hour shift on an observed holiday, total pay for that day is $200
(8 hours x $25 standard holiday pay) plus $300 (8 hours x $37.50 premium
rate) for hours actually worked, for a combined $500, reflecting both the
holiday pay itself and the 1.5x premium for hours worked. Salaried
exempt employees who work on a holiday do not receive additional premium
pay, consistent with their exempt status.

## Compensatory Time for Exempt On-Call Staff

Exempt (salaried) employees who are required to actively work a
significant portion of an observed holiday due to an operational need
(such as a production incident) may, with manager approval, take a
compensatory day off within 30 days in lieu of the premium pay that
non-exempt hourly employees receive, since exempt employees are not
eligible for overtime-style premium pay under the Fair Labor Standards
Act.

## Holiday Calendar Publication

HR publishes the following calendar year's complete holiday schedule,
including specific dates, floating holiday guidance, and any known
regional variations, by November 1st of the preceding year, distributed
via email and posted to the HR portal. Employees who need to plan
travel or major PTO requests around known holidays should reference this
published calendar rather than assuming holiday dates in advance of
publication.

## Interaction with Leave of Absence

Employees on an approved unpaid Leave of Absence do not receive holiday
pay for holidays that fall entirely within the unpaid leave period,
since holiday pay is a form of active-employment compensation. Employees
on a paid Leave of Absence (such as the paid portion of parental leave
described in the Benefits Overview) continue to receive holiday pay for
any holiday falling within that paid period, consistent with the
overlap treatment described above.

## Multi-Year Planning and Historical Reference

HR maintains a rolling 3-year forward calendar (current year plus the
next 2 years) on the HR portal so that teams can plan major
project milestones, product launches, and travel around known holiday
dates well in advance. Historical holiday calendars for the prior 5
years are also retained for payroll audit purposes, since holiday pay
calculations occasionally need to be reconciled against a specific past
pay period.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does a floating holiday need to be tied to a specific religious or
personal occasion?** No; employees may use a floating holiday for any
purpose and are not required to disclose the reason when scheduling it
through the HR portal.

**What happens if I am hired on the day of a holiday?** New hires whose
first day coincides with an observed holiday begin holiday-pay
eligibility the following business day and are not paid for the holiday
itself, consistent with the New Hire Holiday Eligibility section above.

**Can I work through the winter closure instead of taking the days
off?** Only employees in essential roles designated in advance by their
department head may work through the winter closure; all other
employees are expected to be offline during the closure period.
